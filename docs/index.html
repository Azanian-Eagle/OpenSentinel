The Solution: Behavioural Sovereignty

OpenSentinel shifts the paradigm from "Challenge-Response" to "Passive Observation". We verify humanity by analysing how you interact, not what you identify.

1. Passive Telemetry

Our ultra-lightweight sensor (<20kb) observes mouse linearity, speed variance, and keystroke dynamics. Humans are imperfect; bots are precise.

2. Local Analysis

We process data locally where possible. We do not need your identity, only the "shape" of your interaction. This ensures POPIA and GDPR compliance.

3. Rust Performance

The backend is built in Rust (Actix-web), offering blazing fast verification speeds and memory safety that legacy systems cannot match.

Introducing Phase 2: AI Integration

Next-Generation Bot Detection: OpenSentinel is proud to announce the successful integration of our Phase 2 ONNX machine-learning model. We have evolved beyond simple linear heuristics to true behavioural variance analysis.

By training a Random Forest classifier on vast datasets of synthetic human and procedural bot telemetry, our Rust-powered backend (utilising the highly-performant `ort` and `ndarray` crates) can now accurately predict bot probabilities in real-time. This lightweight model analyses cursor deviation, speed variance, and keystroke dynamics with remarkable precision.

True Privacy-Preserving AI: Most importantly, our model is bundled locally with the server. Your users' interactions are analysed instantaneously on your own infrastructure. No telemetry is sent to third-party cloud providers, and we never train our models on your users' unconsented labour.

Regulations and Compliance

Built for the Privacy Era: In a regulatory environment increasingly focused on user data protection, OpenSentinel is engineered from the ground up to ensure strict compliance with global privacy standards, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) of South Africa.

Unlike traditional verification systems that rely on invasive browser fingerprinting, cross-site tracking cookies, and the harvesting of personal data, OpenSentinel operates exclusively on anonymous, session-based behavioural telemetry. We measure the physics of interaction—such as cursor velocity, trajectory variance, and keystroke timing—without ever tying this data to an identifiable individual or device profile.

Because data processing occurs locally through your self-hosted instance and our on-device ONNX models, no sensitive user information is ever transmitted to or stored by third-party cloud providers. This architectural commitment guarantees that your platform remains fully compliant while respecting the digital rights and dignity of your users.

Phase 3: The Decentralised Sentinel Network

Strength in Numbers, Privacy in Architecture: The greatest advantage of centralised systems like Cloudflare Turnstile is their vast network intelligence. OpenSentinel achieves this same collective immunity through a Federated P2P Threat Intelligence Network.

Anonymised Signatures

Nodes automatically share abstract mathematical representations of novel bot behaviours. No IP addresses or user data ever leave your server.

Resilient Client Fallback

Our robust `sensor.js` is network-aware. If your primary Rust backend is unreachable, the client seamlessly fails over to trusted peer nodes in your configuration.

Continuous Evolution

As nodes encounter new threats, the collective dataset is utilised to retrain and update the local ONNX machine-learning definitions, keeping the entire ecosystem ahead of the curve.

Ready to Integrate?

Implementation is simple, but powerful. Our client is built for high availability and robust security.

integration.html 
<script src="sensor.js"></script>
<script>
  // Initialize OpenSentinel with network awareness
  OpenSentinel.init({
    // Primary backend
    endpoints: ['https://api.yourdomain.com/verify', 'https://fallback.trust-node.com/verify'],

    // Optional: Enable client-side Proof-of-Work to deter script abuse
    enablePoW: true,

    // Optional: Register callbacks
    onSuccess: function(token) { console.log('Human verified'); },
    onFailure: function() { console.log('Bot detected'); }
  });
</script>
